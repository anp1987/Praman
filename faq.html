General

Compliance Basics

What is data protection compliance?

Data protection compliance means ensuring your organisation collects, processes, stores, and shares personal data in accordance with applicable laws and regulations. It involves implementing appropriate technical and organisational measures to safeguard individuals' privacy rights.

Which compliance frameworks does Praman cover?

We provide advisory services across multiple frameworks including India's DPDP Act, the EU's GDPR, ISO 27001, SOC 2, HIPAA (for healthcare data), PCI DSS (for payment data), and other sector-specific regulations. Our approach helps you build a unified compliance posture rather than siloed efforts.

Do I need to comply with multiple frameworks at once?

It depends on your business. If you process data of EU residents, you need GDPR compliance alongside DPDP. If you handle health data, HIPAA applies. If your clients demand security certifications, ISO 27001 or SOC 2 may be required. We help you identify which frameworks apply and build an integrated compliance programme.

What's the difference between compliance and certification?

Compliance means meeting the requirements of a law or standard. Certification (like ISO 27001 or SOC 2) is a formal attestation by an independent auditor that you meet those requirements. Some frameworks (DPDP, GDPR) don't have formal certification — you simply need to demonstrate compliance if questioned.

How long does it take to become compliant?

Timelines vary by framework and your current maturity. A DPDP readiness assessment takes 2–3 weeks; full implementation is typically 6–10 weeks. ISO 27001 certification can take 3–6 months. SOC 2 readiness usually requires 2–4 months. We'll give you a realistic timeline during the scoping call.

What happens if we're not compliant?

Consequences range from financial penalties (up to ₹250 crore under DPDP, up to €20M or 4% of global turnover under GDPR) to reputational damage, loss of customer trust, and business restrictions. Many enterprise clients now require compliance proof before signing contracts.

India

DPDP Act (Digital Personal Data Protection)

What is the DPDP Act, 2023?

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 is India's comprehensive data protection law. It governs how organisations (called Data Fiduciaries) collect, process, and store personal data of individuals (Data Principals) in India, establishing rights for individuals and obligations for businesses.

Who does the DPDP Act apply to?

It applies to any organisation that processes digital personal data collected within India, or processes personal data of individuals in India even if the processing happens outside the country. This covers startups, SMBs, enterprises, and foreign companies serving Indian users.

What are the key obligations under DPDP?

Key obligations include: obtaining valid consent before processing data, providing clear privacy notices, enabling data principal rights (access, correction, erasure), appointing a Data Protection Officer for significant data fiduciaries, implementing reasonable security safeguards, reporting breaches to the Data Protection Board, and ensuring data processors comply through contracts.

What is a "Consent Manager" under DPDP?

A Consent Manager is a registered entity that acts as an intermediary between Data Principals and Data Fiduciaries, helping individuals manage their consent preferences across multiple services. They must be registered with the Data Protection Board of India.

What are the penalties for non-compliance with DPDP?

Penalties can reach up to ₹250 crore per instance depending on the violation. Specific penalties apply for different breaches — failure to prevent data breaches, non-compliance with obligations regarding children's data, and failure to notify the Board of breaches all carry significant fines.

Does DPDP apply to employee data?

Yes. Any personal data you process — whether of customers, employees, vendors, or partners — falls under DPDP if it's digital personal data collected in India. HR systems, payroll, attendance records, and employee monitoring all need to comply.

How does DPDP handle cross-border data transfers?

DPDP allows cross-border transfers to all countries except those specifically restricted by the Central Government via notification. Unlike GDPR's adequacy mechanism, India uses a "blacklist" approach — transfers are permitted unless the destination country is explicitly blocked.

Europe

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

What is GDPR and does it apply to Indian companies?

GDPR is the European Union's data protection regulation. It applies to any organisation — regardless of location — that offers goods or services to, or monitors the behaviour of, individuals in the EU/EEA. If you have European customers or users, GDPR likely applies to you.

How is GDPR different from DPDP?

While both protect personal data, GDPR is broader in scope: it has six legal bases for processing (not just consent), stricter cross-border transfer rules (adequacy decisions, SCCs, BCRs), mandatory DPO requirements for certain processors, and the right to data portability. DPDP is simpler but with heavier penalties relative to its scope.

What are the lawful bases for processing under GDPR?

GDPR provides six lawful bases: consent, contractual necessity, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, and legitimate interests. Unlike DPDP which primarily relies on consent and "certain legitimate uses," GDPR gives you more flexibility in justifying data processing.

Do we need a representative in the EU?

If your organisation is outside the EU but processes EU residents' data, you generally need to appoint an EU representative under Article 27 — unless processing is occasional, doesn't include large-scale special category data, and is unlikely to result in risk to individuals' rights.

What is a DPIA and when do we need one?

A Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) is required under GDPR when processing is likely to result in high risk to individuals — such as large-scale profiling, systematic monitoring of public areas, or processing sensitive data at scale. It's a structured risk assessment with mitigation measures.

Security

ISO 27001

What is ISO 27001?

ISO 27001 is an international standard for Information Security Management Systems (ISMS). It provides a framework for managing information security risks through policies, processes, and controls. Certification demonstrates to clients and partners that you take security seriously.

How does ISO 27001 relate to data protection?

ISO 27001 covers the "security" side of data protection — ensuring confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information. While DPDP/GDPR focus on privacy rights and lawful processing, ISO 27001 ensures the technical and organisational controls protecting that data are robust. They complement each other.

How long does ISO 27001 certification take?

For a mid-size company, expect 3–6 months from gap assessment to certification audit. This includes defining scope, implementing controls, building documentation, running internal audits, and finally the Stage 1 and Stage 2 external audits.

Is ISO 27001 mandatory?

It's not legally mandatory in most jurisdictions. However, it's increasingly required by enterprise clients, government contracts, and as a prerequisite for processing certain types of data. Many RFPs now list it as a must-have. It also supports DPDP's requirement for "reasonable security safeguards."

What is the ISO 27701 extension?

ISO 27701 extends ISO 27001 specifically for privacy information management. It maps to GDPR requirements and provides a certifiable privacy framework. If you already have ISO 27001, adding 27701 is a natural next step to demonstrate privacy compliance.

Trust

SOC 2

What is SOC 2?

SOC 2 (System and Organisation Controls 2) is an auditing standard developed by the AICPA. It evaluates an organisation's controls related to security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. It's particularly relevant for SaaS companies and service providers.

What's the difference between SOC 2 Type I and Type II?

Type I assesses whether your controls are properly designed at a specific point in time. Type II evaluates whether those controls operated effectively over a period (typically 3–12 months). Type II is more rigorous and more valued by enterprise clients.

Do we need SOC 2 if we already have ISO 27001?

They serve different audiences. ISO 27001 is globally recognised, while SOC 2 is preferred by US-based clients. Many companies pursuing US enterprise deals need SOC 2 specifically. There's significant overlap in controls, so having one makes achieving the other easier.

How long does SOC 2 readiness take?

SOC 2 readiness typically takes 2–4 months, depending on your current security posture. The observation period for Type II adds another 3–6 months. We help you implement controls and prepare evidence so the audit process goes smoothly.

Healthcare

HIPAA

What is HIPAA and when does it apply?

HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) is a US law protecting health information. It applies to healthcare providers, health plans, healthcare clearinghouses (covered entities), and their service providers (business associates). If you handle Protected Health Information (PHI) for US healthcare clients, HIPAA applies.

Does HIPAA apply to Indian companies?

Yes, if you're a business associate of a US covered entity — for example, if you provide IT services, cloud hosting, analytics, or BPO services that involve accessing PHI. Many Indian health-tech and IT services companies serving US healthcare clients must comply with HIPAA.

What are the key HIPAA requirements?

HIPAA has three main rules: the Privacy Rule (how PHI can be used/disclosed), the Security Rule (technical, physical, and administrative safeguards for electronic PHI), and the Breach Notification Rule (reporting requirements when PHI is compromised). You also need Business Associate Agreements with covered entities.

Is there a HIPAA certification?

Unlike ISO 27001, there is no official HIPAA certification. Compliance is demonstrated through risk assessments, documented policies, training records, and audit trails. Third-party assessments can provide attestation, but the government doesn't certify organisations as "HIPAA compliant."

Payments

PCI DSS

What is PCI DSS?

PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard) is a set of security requirements for organisations that handle credit/debit card data. It's mandated by card brands (Visa, Mastercard, etc.) and applies to merchants, payment processors, and service providers handling cardholder data.

Do we need PCI DSS if we use a payment gateway?

If you use a fully hosted payment gateway (like Razorpay, Stripe, or PayU) and never see or store card numbers, your PCI scope is minimal — typically a Self-Assessment Questionnaire (SAQ A). But you still have some obligations around secure redirects and page security.

How does PCI DSS interact with DPDP?

Card data is personal data under DPDP. PCI DSS compliance helps meet DPDP's "reasonable security safeguards" requirement for payment data. However, DPDP adds consent, rights, and notice obligations that PCI DSS doesn't cover. You need both.
